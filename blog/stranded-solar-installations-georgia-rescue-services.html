What Are Stranded Solar Installations?

A stranded solar installation refers to a solar energy system that is either completely non-functional, partially working, or abandoned by the original installer. These situations leave homeowners frustrated, financially burdened, and without the energy savings they were promised.

Warning Signs of a Stranded Installation:
  • Solar panels installed but never connected to your electrical system
  • System that worked initially but stopped producing energy
  • Installation left incomplete with no contractor follow-up
  • Equipment failures with no warranty support available
  • Installer company has gone out of business or become unresponsive

Common Causes of Stranded Solar Systems in Georgia

1. Contractor Business Failures

Unfortunately, not all solar companies survive in the competitive market. When installers go out of business, homeowners are left with systems that may be incomplete, improperly installed, or without warranty support.

2. Poor Quality Installations

Some contractors cut corners to maximize profits, resulting in systems that fail prematurely or never work correctly. Common issues include:

  • Improper wiring and electrical connections
  • Inadequate mounting systems
  • Wrong equipment sizing
  • Failure to obtain proper permits
  • Non-compliance with local building codes

3. Equipment Failures and Warranty Issues

When solar equipment fails and the original installer cannot honor warranties, homeowners face expensive repairs with no clear path forward.

4. Incomplete Installations

Some installations are started but never finished due to various reasons including permitting issues, contractor cash flow problems, or disputes between homeowners and installers.

Prevention: How to Avoid Stranded Solar Installations

While we're here to help rescue stranded systems, prevention is always better than cure. Here's how to protect yourself:

Research Your Installer Thoroughly

  • Verify Georgia contractor license and insurance
  • Check Better Business Bureau ratings and reviews
  • Ask for local references and visit completed installations
  • Ensure they have NABCEP certified installers
  • Verify they've been in business for several years

Understand Your Contract

  • Read all terms carefully before signing
  • Ensure warranties are clearly defined
  • Verify permit and inspection responsibilities
  • Understand payment schedule and milestones
  • Ensure completion timeline is realistic

Monitor Installation Progress

  • Verify permits are obtained before work begins
  • Ensure all work passes required inspections
  • Don't make final payment until system is fully operational
  • Get all documentation including warranties and manuals

Georgia-Specific Considerations

State Regulations and Codes

Georgia has specific requirements for solar installations that must be met:

  • Electrical contractor license required for all solar work
  • Building permits required for most installations
  • Compliance with Georgia Building and Fire Codes
  • Utility interconnection agreements
  • Net metering program participation

Climate and Environmental Factors

Georgia's climate presents unique challenges:

  • Hurricane and severe weather resistance requirements
  • High humidity and corrosion considerations
  • Proper drainage and mounting for heavy rain
  • Heat management for optimal performance

Utility Interconnection

Different Georgia utilities have varying requirements:

  • Georgia Power interconnection procedures
  • EMC (Electric Membership Corporation) requirements
  • Municipal utility procedures
  • Net metering application processes
